"I believe good design solves business problems, not just visual problems."

I got into design almost by accident. I was studying Computer Science and kept gravitating toward the parts of a build where the interface didn't quite work for the person using it. Fixing that turned out to be the part I liked most.

What excites me now is the handoff between research and interface: taking what real users actually do, and turning it into a flow that feels obvious in hindsight. I enjoy working with startups and small teams especially, where design decisions are visible quickly, not months later.

I've worked across fintech, SaaS, consumer apps and retail brands: different industries, same question every time. What does this business need this screen to do?
